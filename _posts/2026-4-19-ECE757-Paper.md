## Abstract

As System-on-Chip (SoC) designs grow in complexity with multiprocessing and heterogeneous computing, integrating third-party hardware  intellectual property (IP) has become a standard practice to accelerate development. However, this reliance on external IP introduces significant security vulnerabilities. Since a Network-on-Chip (NoC) connects multiple processors, the NoC is an attractive target for hardware Trojans (HT), malicious modifications at the hardware level that compromise data integrity and waste system resources. Designing robust and secure measures analogous to traditional network security is essential to mitigate HTs. This paper proposes a pseudo-random routing algorithm that dynamically changes the edge weights between routers, which may cause two identical transactions to take different paths. By changing the edge weights on the fly, a packet in the network may be rerouted at each step, obfuscating the path from attackers. By leveraging pseudo-random network rerouting, we aim to mitigate HTs with high probability while introducing acceptable area and power overheads and scale efficiently to larger NoCs.
